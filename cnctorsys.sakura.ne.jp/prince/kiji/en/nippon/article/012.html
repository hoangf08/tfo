▲View of Ginowan City, Okinawa from PIXTA

Only 30 minutes by car from Naha. Located in the center of Okinawa Island, Ginowan City is a bridge between the north and south, and is a recommended tourist spot in the central Okinawa region.

Ginowan City, located in the center of Okinawa's main island, is an attractive place with beautiful seas and abundant nature. It is an area with good access, about 30 minutes by car from Naha Airport under normal circumstances. It is also an ideal base for sightseeing in Okinawa, as it is easy to get to both the southern and northern parts of Okinawa.
In this article, we will introduce recommended spots, gourmet food, and events in Ginowan City.

Recommended spots

Active! Recommended sightseeing spots in Ginowan City

  1. Recommended spot 1

    A mysterious shrine with a limestone cave

    1 Futenmangu Shrine

    • ▲"Futenmangu Shrine" from PIXTA

    • ▲"Futenmangu Shrine" from PIXTA

    Futenma Shrine is one of the eight Ryukyu Okinawa shrines.
    The grounds contain the 280-meter-long Futenma Shrine Cave, a city-designated cultural property.
    The cave and the area around the east entrance are ruins, and many artifacts from the latter half of the early Okinawa shell mound period (about 3,000 years ago) have been excavated.

    Futenmangu Shrine

    A panoramic view of beautiful Okinawa from the observation deck

    2 Kakazu Takadai Park

    • ▲"Kakazu Takadai Park" from PIXTA

    • ▲"Kakazu Takadai Park" from PIXTA

  3. Recommended spot 3

    Enjoy the sea on a beautiful beach

    3 Tropical Beach | Ginowan Seaside Park

    • ▲"Ginowan Seaside Park" from PIXTA

    • ▲"Ginowan Seaside Park" from PIXTA

    Ginowan Seaside Park's tropical beach is characterized by its white sand and crystal clear blue water, and its beauty is unforgettable once you visit. You can enjoy activities such as snorkeling and beach volleyball, as well as barbecues.
    Coin lockers, coin showers, and many benches are also available, making it a well-equipped facility. The benches along the beach are perfect for spending a relaxing time with family and friends. At sunset, you can see the beautiful view of the sun sinking into the sea, creating a romantic atmosphere.

  5. Recommended spot 5

    A short trip from Ginowan to a World Heritage site with a magnificent view

    5 Katsuren Castle Ruins

    • ▲"Katsuren Castle Ruins" from PIXTA

    • ▲"Katsuren Castle Ruins, Uruma City, Okinawa Prefecture" From PIXTA

    • ▲"Katsuren Castle Ruins (Okinawa)" From PIXTA

    Katsuren Castle Ruins is a highly recommended tourist spot that can be accessed by car from Ginowan City in about 30 to 40 minutes under normal circumstances.
    Katsuren Castle is known as the castle of Amawari, an anji (one of the official titles in the Ryukyu Islands before the Meiji era) who held over the Katsuren Peninsula during the Ryukyu Dynasty. It was built in the 15th century, the castle's beautiful curves, taking advantage of the hilly terrain, were registered as a World Heritage Site in 2000.

    The view of Okinawa's beautiful ocean from the top of the castle is spectacular.

Recommended Events

Experience local culture: Events in Ginowan City

  1. Recommended Event 1

    The earliest summer in Japan! 10,000 fireworks with spectacular sight

    1 Ryukyu Sea Flame Festival

    • ▲"Ryukyu Sea Flame Festival" from PIXTA

    • ▲"Ryukyu Sea Flame Festival" from PIXTA

    The Ryukyu Sea Flame Festival is a traditional seaside fireworks display that has been held for over 20 years under the theme of "The earliest moving summer in Japan." It is one of the largest entertainment events in Okinawa, with folk songs and dances also being performed.

    It is a world-class fireworks display, with artistic fireworks designed by world-renowned designer Junko Koshino.

    Ryukyu Sea Flame Festival

    Event Location
    Ginowan Seaside Park (for 2024)
    Event Date
    Saturday, April 13th (for 2024)
    Official Website Google Maps

  2. Recommended Event 2

    A festival named after the Hagoromo legend

    2 Ginowan Hagoromo Festival

    • ▲"Fireworks at the Hagoromo Festival in Ginowan" from PIXTA

    The Ginowan Hagoromo Festival is a traditional festival named after the legend that a heavenly maiden descended from the earth and took a bath there.

    A gorgeous portable shrine is decorated with flowers and ornaments, and various events are held to enliven the festival, such as a food stall street where you can enjoy food stalls and local cuisine, and a Civic Cultural Center where local performing arts and traditional crafts are exhibited.

    Ginowan Hagoromo Festival

  3. Recommended Event 3

    Okinawa's traditional eisa performing arts

    3 Okinawa City Youth Eisa Festival

    • ▲"Okinawa Eisa Festival" from PIXTA

    The Okinawa City Youth Eisa Festival is a large-scale festival for enjoying Okinawa's traditional dance, "Eisa." The powerful Eisa dance performance by local young people is a spectacular sight! Eisa is a traditional dance performed to the rhythm of the drums, and the dancers wear colorful costumes and perform a passionate performance. At the end of the festival, the audience joins in the dance, bringing the festival to a close.

    Okinawa City Youth Eisa Festival

    Event Location
    Ginowan Seaside Park Multipurpose Square (for 2024)

  4. Recommended Event 4

    A traditional precious Japanese event held at the sea

    4 Ginowan Hagoromo Hari Tournament

    • ▲"Ginowan Hagoromo Hari Tournament" provided by the on-site office representative

    • ▲"Dragon Boat" from PIXTA

    "Hāli" is a traditional Okinawan event in which participants pray for safe voyages and bountiful catches and race in "dragon boats" decorated with a dragon head on the bow and a dragon tail on the stern, or "sabani," traditional Okinawan fishing boats.
    It is held in various parts of Okinawa, including Naha, Itoman, and Kadena.

    Ginowan will also host the "Ginowan Hagoromo Hari Tournament" in 2024, and is scheduled to hold it around the same time in 2025. There are also plans for a Hari trial event to be held, so you may have a chance to experience this traditional Okinawan event for yourself.

    Ginowan Hagoromo Hari Tournament

    Event Location
    Ginowan Port Marina (for 2024)
    Event Date
    Sunday, July 7, 2024 (for 2024)
